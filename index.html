Mastering Introductions

Learn the 20% of language that gives you 80% of the results.

Key Vocabulary

Category A: Identity & Origins

Hometown (مسقط رأس): The city or town where you were born or grew up.
  • My hometown is a small village near the coast.
  • I haven't visited my hometown in five years.
  • Is Cairo your hometown?
Based in (مقرّه في / مقيم في): Living or working in a specific place currently.
  • I'm originally from Italy, but I'm based in London.
  • Our company is based in the city center.
  • Where are you based these days?

Category B: Roles & Interests

Current (حالي): Happening or existing now.
  • My current job is very stressful.
  • What is your current address?
  • I'm happy with my current situation.
Into (مهتم بـ): To be very interested in or enjoy something.
  • I'm really into photography lately.
  • Are you into sports?
  • He is into jazz music and classical guitar.

Core Expressions

"I'm [Name], by the way."

Context: Used to introduce yourself in the middle of a conversation if you forgot at the start.

  • "Great weather today! I'm Alex, by the way."
  • "I love this song. I'm Sarah, by the way."
  • "Are you new here? I'm Mark, by the way."
"What brings you here?"

Context: A polite way to ask why someone is at an event or location.

  • "Hi! What brings you to this conference?"
  • "Nice to meet you. What brings you to Dubai?"
  • "What brings you here so early?"
6 more expressions included in full plan: "Nice to meet you," "I've heard a lot about you," "How about you?", "What do you do for a living?", "I'm originally from...", "Actually, I..."

Real-Life Dialogues

1. The Coffee Shop
A: Hi there! Is this seat taken?
B: No, go ahead. I'm Sarah, by the way.
A: Nice to meet you, Sarah. I'm Ben.
B: Nice to meet you! What brings you here today?
A: Just working on my laptop. I'm a freelance writer.
B: Oh, cool! I'm into writing as well.
2. The Business Meeting
A: Hello, you must be Mr. Ahmed.
B: Yes, that's right. I've heard a lot about your project.
A: All good things, I hope! I'm the lead designer.
B: Great. I'm currently based in the Riyadh office.
A: Welcome! Have you been working there long?
B: About three years now.

Common Mistakes

1. I have 20 years old.

1. I am 20 years old.

Why? In English, we use "to be" for age, not "to have."

2. I am student.

2. I am a student.

Why? Always use the article "a/an" before singular professions.

3. I am coming from Spain.

3. I come from Spain / I'm from Spain.

Why? Use Present Simple for permanent facts like your origin.

4. How do you do? (to mean 'How are you?')

4. How are you? / How's it going?

Why? "How do you do" is a very formal greeting only used when meeting for the first time, not as a general question.

5. Nice to meet you. (Response: Nice to meet you too.)

5. Nice to meet you. (Response: You too! / Likewise.)

Why? While not "wrong," "You too" is more natural in modern 80/20 English.
