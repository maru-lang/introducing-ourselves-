The Art of Introduction

Master 80% of social interactions with 20% of the effort.

Key Vocabulary (80/20 Rule)

Category 1: Openers & Greetings

Nice to meet you (تشرفنا / سررت بلقائك): A polite phrase used when meeting someone for the first time.
  • It's a pleasure to be here. Nice to meet you.
  • Nice to meet you, I've heard great things about your work.
  • A: "I'm Sarah." B: "Nice to meet you, Sarah!"
By the way (بالمناسبة): Used to introduce a new topic or provide extra information (like your name).
  • I'm Omar, by the way.
  • By the way, do you know where the library is?
  • That's a great bag! I'm Layla, by the way.

Category 2: Context & Background

Based in (مستقر في): Where you currently live or where your office is located.
  • I'm currently based in Dubai.
  • Our team is based in London, but we work remotely.
  • Where are you based at the moment?
Into (مهتم بـ / أحب): Very interested in a hobby or activity.
  • I'm really into graphic design.
  • What kind of music are you into?
  • I'm into hiking and outdoor photography.

Essential Expressions

"I've heard a lot about you." (لقد سمعت الكثير عنك)

Context: Meeting someone your friends or colleagues have mentioned before.

  • "You're the new manager? I've heard a lot about you!"
  • "Finally meeting! I've heard a lot about you from Mark."
  • "I've heard a lot about you. It's great to put a face to the name."
"What brings you here today?" (ما الذي أتى بك إلى هنا اليوم؟)

Context: A great conversation starter at parties, events, or meetings.

  • "Hi! What brings you to this workshop today?"
  • "It's a beautiful day. What brings you to the park?"
  • "What brings you here? Are you a friend of the host?"
"What do you do for a living?" (ماذا تعمل؟)

Context: Asking about someone's job or profession.

  • "So, what do you do for a living?"
  • "I'm a designer. What do you do for a living?"
  • "I'm curious, what do you do for a living?"
"How about you?" / "And yourself?" (ماذا عنك؟ / وأنت؟)

Context: Returning a question to keep the conversation balanced.

  • "I'm from New York. How about you?"
  • "I work in finance. And yourself?"
  • "I'm into jazz. How about you?"
Other Key Expressions: "I’m currently working as a...", "How do you know [Name]?", "Have you been working there long?", "Actually, I..."

Natural Dialogues

1. Casual Meeting at a Park
A: Hi! I love your tote bag.
B: Oh, thank you! I'm Maya, by the way.
A: Nice to meet you, Maya. I'm Chloe.
B: Nice to meet you too! What brings you here today?
A: I'm just enjoying the sun. I'm originally from London, so I'm not used to this heat!
B: Haha! I understand. How about you? Are you based here?
2. Professional Networking
A: Hello, I'm Omar. I'm currently working as a graphic designer.
B: Nice to meet you, Omar. I've heard a lot about your work.
A: Oh, really? That's kind. And what do you do for a living?
B: I'm in marketing. I'm based in Riyadh but I'm here for the conference.
A: That's great. Have you been working there long?
B: About five years now. It's a busy job!
3. Meeting a Friend's Friend
A: Hey! You must be Sarah. I'm Khalid.
B: Hi Khalid! Yes, I'm Sarah. How do you know Ahmed?
A: We went to university together. What brings you to his party?
B: We work together. I'm a software engineer.
A: Cool! I'm actually into coding too. How about you, do you like the job?
B: I love it, though it can be stressful sometimes.
4. Quick Coffee Shop Chat
A: Is this seat taken? I'm Leo, by the way.
B: No, go ahead. I'm Hana. Nice to meet you.
A: Nice to meet you, Hana. What do you do for a living?
B: I'm a student. I'm currently studying English pedagogy.
A: That sounds interesting! Are you based in this city?
B: Yes, I've lived here for three years. How about you?

Common Mistakes (Fix Them!)

1. I have 25 years.

1. I am 25 / I am 25 years old.

Explanation: In English, we use the verb 'to be' for age, not 'to have'.

2. I am student.

2. I am a student.

Explanation: You must use 'a' or 'an' before your profession/job title.

3. I'm work as a doctor.

3. I work as a doctor / I'm a doctor.

Explanation: Don't mix 'am' with a base verb. Use 'I work' or 'I am a...'.

4. How do you call this?

4. What do you call this?

Explanation: When asking for a name or label, use 'What', not 'How'.

5. I live here since two years.

5. I have lived here for two years.

Explanation: Use 'for' with a duration of time and 'Present Perfect' for actions starting in the past.

Open-Ended Discussion

  1. Introduce yourself in 3 sentences using the "Elevator Pitch" method.
    Hi, I'm Omar. I work as a designer and I'm based in Meknes. I'm really into football, how about you?
  2. How would you introduce yourself at a professional job interview?
    Good morning. It's nice to meet you. I am currently working as a project manager and have five years of experience.
  3. Someone says "I've heard a lot about you." How do you respond?
    "Oh, really? All good things, I hope! Nice to finally meet you too."
  4. Ask three different people what they "do for a living" using different phrases.
    1. What do you do for a living? 2. What's your current role? 3. What do you do?
  5. Talk about your hometown and where you are based now.
    I'm originally from a small town in the north, but I'm based in the capital city now.
