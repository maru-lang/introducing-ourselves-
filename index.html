Let's Introduce Ourselves

Expert ESL Curriculum • 80/20 Rule & Input/Output Method

Core Vocabulary (12 Words)

1. Surname (اسم العائلة)

Your last name or family name.

  • My surname is Mansouri.
  • Please write your first name and surname.
  • Is your surname spelled with one 'S' or two?

2. Hometown (مسقط الرأس)

The city or town where you were born.

  • Meknes is my hometown.
  • What is your hometown famous for?
  • I visited my hometown last summer.

3. Siblings (الأشقاء)

Your brothers and sisters.

  • I have three siblings.
  • Do you have any siblings in this school?
  • My siblings and I love playing football.

4. Hobby (هواية)

An activity you do for fun in your free time.

  • My favorite hobby is drawing.
  • Reading is a very healthy hobby.
  • I need to find a new hobby for the weekend.

5. Originally (في الأصل)

From the beginning; where you started.

  • I live in Rabat, but I'm originally from Tangier.
  • She is originally from France.
  • Where are your parents originally from?

6. Nickname (لقب)

A short or funny name used by friends.

  • My nickname is "The Flash" because I am fast.
  • Does your teacher have a nickname?
  • You can call me by my nickname, Sam.

7. Grade (مستوى دراسي)

The level of a student in school.

  • I am in the 6th grade.
  • Which grade are you in?
  • My brother is in the 4th grade.

8. Introduce (يُعرف)

To tell someone your name for the first time.

  • Let me introduce myself.
  • Can you introduce your friend to us?
  • I feel shy when I introduce myself to new people.

9. Neighbor (جار)

A person living next door to you.

  • My neighbor is very kind.
  • I play games with my neighbor every day.
  • We have a new neighbor moving in today.

10. Interest (اهتمام)

Something you want to learn or know about.

  • I have an interest in science.
  • What are your main interests?
  • Music is my biggest interest.

11. Pleasure (سرور)

A feeling of happy satisfaction.

  • It is a pleasure to meet you.
  • Helping you is a pleasure.
  • Reading gives me great pleasure.

12. Subject (مادة دراسية)

An area of study in school like Math or English.

  • My favorite subject is English.
  • How many subjects do you study?
  • Math is a difficult subject for me.

Common Phrases & Usage

1. "Let me introduce myself." (اسمح لي أن أعرف بنفسي)

Context: Use this to start a formal conversation with a stranger.

  • Example: Let me introduce myself. My name is Omar.
  • Example: Hello class, let me introduce myself. I am your new teacher.
  • Example: Excuse me, let me introduce myself before we start.

2. "I'm into..." (أنا مهتم بـ)

Context: A natural way to talk about your hobbies or interests.

  • Example: I'm really into football.
  • Example: Are you into drawing like I am?
  • Example: I'm not really into video games.

3. "You can call me..." (يمكنك مناداتي بـ)

Context: Use this when you have a nickname you prefer.

  • Example: My name is Alexander, but you can call me Alex.
  • Example: You can call me "The Captain" during the game!
  • Example: Please, you can call me by my first name.

4. "Pleased to meet you." (سرني لقاؤك)

Context: Use this to show pleasure when meeting someone for the first time.

  • Example: Hello Sarah! Pleased to meet you.
  • Example: It was a pleasure to meet you, Mr. Ahmed.
  • Example: Pleased to meet you too!

Real-Life Dialogues

Ali: Hello! Let me introduce myself. I'm Ali.

Sami: Hi Ali! I'm Sami. You can call me "Sam."

Sami: Yes, I'm originally from Agadir, but I moved here last week.

Ali: Welcome! Do you have any siblings in our school?

Sami: No, I'm an only child. I'm really into making new friends, though!

Sara: Yes, it is! How did you know?

Mona: I'm your new neighbor. I live in the apartment next to yours.

Sara: Oh! Nice to finally meet you, Mona.

Mona: Nice to meet you too. What's your favorite subject at school?

Sara: I love English! It's my favorite hobby too.

Kamal: I'm into computer science and playing basketball.

Avoid These Errors

I am have 11 years old.

I am 11 years old.

Explanation: In English, we use the verb 'to be' for age, not 'have'.

My nickname it is "Tiger".

My nickname is "Tiger".

Explanation: Do not use 'it' after the subject. "My nickname" is already the subject.

Nice to meet your.

Nice to meet you.

Explanation: 'Your' is for possession. 'You' is the person you are meeting.

I am from Casablanca originally from.

I am originally from Casablanca.

Explanation: 'Originally' usually comes before the place or after 'am'.

I'm interest in Math.

I'm interested in Math. / I have an interest in Math.

Explanation: Use the adjective 'interested' or the noun 'interest'.

Image Practice & Discussion

1. Describe what you see:

Clue: "The boy is..."

Example: The boy is introducing himself and waving hello.

2. How do you go to school?

Clue: "I go to school by..."

Example: I go to school by bus every day.

3. Tell me about your siblings:

Clue: "I have..."

Example: I have two siblings, one brother and one sister.

Discussion Questions

1. What is your full name and surname?

Example: My full name is Sarah Alami.

2. What is your favorite subject and why?

Example: I like English because I enjoy learning new words.

3. Do you have a nickname? Who gave it to you?

Example: My nickname is Leo. My friends gave it to me because I like lions.

4. Where is your hometown? What is it like?

Example: My hometown is Ifrane. It is very cold and beautiful in winter.

5. What kind of hobbies are you into?

Example: I am into playing video games and swimming.
