Mastering Introductions

6th Grade ESL • 80/20 Method • Input/Output Framework

Essential Vocabulary

Identity (الهوية)

Full Name: The complete name of a person including first and last name.

  • My full name is Ahmed Mansouri.
  • Please write your full name on the paper.
  • I don't know his full name, only his first name.

Hometown (مسقط الرأس)

Definition: The town or city where you were born or grew up.

  • Meknes is my hometown.
  • What is your hometown like?
  • I moved away from my hometown three years ago.

Siblings (الأشقاء)

Definition: Your brothers and sisters.

  • I have three siblings: two brothers and one sister.
  • Do you have any siblings?
  • Being the oldest of four siblings is hard work!

Conversation Power-Ups

1. "I'm originally from..." (أنا في الأصل من...)

Context: Used when you live in one place but were born somewhere else.

  • I live in Rabat now, but I'm originally from Tangier.
  • Where are you originally from?
  • She's originally from France, but her English is perfect.

2. "You can call me..." (يمكنك مناداتي بـ...)

Context: Used to introduce your nickname or a shorter name.

  • My name is Samantha, but you can call me Sam.
  • You can call me Leo if Leonard is too long.
  • Please, you can call me by my first name.

3. "Nice to finally meet you." (سعدت بلقائك أخيراً)

Context: Use this when you have talked online or on the phone before meeting in person.

  • We've emailed so much! Nice to finally meet you.
  • Oh, you're Omar! Nice to finally meet you.
  • It's great to be here. Nice to finally meet you all.

Real-Life Dialogues

Youssef: Hello! I'm Youssef, the new student.

Sarah: Hi Youssef! I'm Sarah. Welcome to our class.

Youssef: Thanks! I'm originally from Marrakesh.

Sarah: That's cool! Do you have any siblings here?

Youssef: Yes, I have a younger sister in Grade 4.

Sarah: Nice. Let me show you where the library is.

Teacher: Good morning class. This is our new guest.

Guest: Hello everyone! My full name is Robert Smith.

Student: Nice to meet you, Mr. Smith. Where are you from?

Guest: I'm from London, but you can call me Rob.

Student: Do you have any hobbies, Rob?

Guest: I'm really into photography and hiking.

Stop Making These Mistakes!

I am live in Casablanca.

I live in Casablanca.

Why: Don't use "am" with action verbs in the present simple.

My name is Ahmed, what is you?

My name is Ahmed, what is yours?

Why: Use the possessive pronoun "yours" to refer to someone's name.

I have 11 years.

I am 11 years old.

Why: In English, age is a state of being (am/is/are), not a possession.

Visual Practice

Q1: What is this person doing?

Pattern: "The person is..."

Example: The person is introducing himself and waving.

Q2: How do you get to school?

Pattern: "I go to school by..."

Example: I go to school by bus every morning.

Discussion Questions

1. What is your favorite thing about your hometown?

Example: My favorite thing is the old market because it smells like spices.

2. Tell me about your siblings or your best friend.

Example: I have one brother. He is younger than me and very funny.
