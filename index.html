Level 6: Introducing Ourselves

Master the 80/20 of English Socializing

The Essentials (80/20 Rule)

Identity & Origins

Hometown (مسقط رأس)

The place where you were born and grew up.

  • My hometown is a small village.
  • What is your hometown like?
  • I visit my hometown every summer.
Background (خلفية / أصل)

Your past experiences or family history.

  • She has a background in science.
  • Tell me about your family background.
  • We come from different backgrounds.
Originally (في الأصل)

Where you started or where you were first.

  • I live in London, but I'm originally from Cairo.
  • Where are you originally from?
  • He is originally a teacher, but now he's an artist.

Daily Life

Current (حالي)

Happening right now at this time.

  • My current job is very exciting.
  • What is your current address?
  • This is my current favorite song.
Hobby (هواية)

An activity you do for fun in your free time.

  • My main hobby is photography.
  • Do you have any unusual hobbies?
  • Painting is a great hobby.
Colleague (زميل عمل)

A person you work with at a job.

  • I’m going to lunch with a colleague.
  • Meet my colleague, Sarah.
  • My colleagues are very helpful.

Common Conversational Phrases

"I'm based in..."

Context: Used to say where you currently live or where your office is.

  • I'm based in Dubai.
  • Our company is based in New York.
  • Where are you based these days?
"Nice to finally meet you in person."

Context: Meeting someone you have only talked to online/email before.

  • It’s great to finally meet you in person, John!
  • We’ve emailed so much, it’s nice to finally meet you in person.
  • Finally, nice to meet you in person!
"I'm into..."

Context: Informal way to describe your interests/hobbies.

  • I'm really into rock climbing.
  • Are you into jazz music?
  • He’s lately been into learning French.
"What do you do for a living?"

Context: A polite way to ask someone what their job is.

  • So, what do you do for a living?
  • I forgot to ask, what do you do for a living?
  • She asked me what I do for a living.

Everyday Situations

1. At a Networking Event

Alex: Hi, I'm Alex. What's your name?
Bea: Nice to meet you, Alex. I'm Bea.
Alex: Nice to meet you too. What brings you here?
Bea: I'm here for the tech talk. I work in IT.
Alex: Interesting! I'm into software development myself.
Bea: Oh, cool! Where are you based?

2. Meeting a Friend's Friend

Tom: Hey, you must be Sam. I'm Tom.
Sam: Hi Tom! Yes, I've heard a lot about you.
Tom: All good things, I hope! How do you know Leo?
Sam: We were colleagues at my last job.
Tom: Small world! I've known him since college.
Sam: That's great. Are you originally from this city?

Avoid These Common Errors

I have 20 years. → I am 20 years old.

Reason: In English, we use "to be" for age, not "to have".

I am living in Riyadh since 5 years. → I have lived in Riyadh for 5 years.

Reason: Use Present Perfect + "for" for a duration of time.

I work like a teacher. → I work as a teacher.

Reason: Use "as" to describe a professional role or job.
