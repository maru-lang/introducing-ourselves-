English Introductions: Native Style

The 80/20 Rule: Learn the phrases native speakers actually use.

Key Vocabulary

Identity & Background

Originally from (أصلي من): Used to talk about your hometown or birth country when living elsewhere.
  • I'm originally from Morocco, but I've lived in London for years.
  • Where are you originally from?
  • She's originally from a small village in the mountains.
Based in (مستقر في): Your current city of residence or work.
  • I'm currently based in Casablanca.
  • The company is based in New York.
  • Are you based here permanently?

Interests & Passion

Big on (محب جداً لـ): Native slang for being a huge fan of something.
  • I'm big on outdoor activities like hiking.
  • Are you big on coffee?
  • He’s not big on social media.
Keen on (متحمس لـ): Common in British English for being interested in something.
  • I'm quite keen on learning new languages.
  • She's keen on joining the marketing team.
  • Are you keen on trying that new restaurant?

Native Conversational Phrases

"I'm [Name], by the way." (بالمناسبة)

Context: The most natural way to introduce yourself mid-conversation.

  • "Great party! I'm Sarah, by the way."
  • "I love your shirt. I'm Mike, by the way."
  • "Are you new to the team? I'm Layla, by the way."
"What brings you here?" (ما الذي أتى بك إلى هنا؟)

Context: Instead of "Why are you here?", natives use this at events or parties.

  • "Hi! What brings you to the conference today?"
  • "What brings you to this part of town?"
  • "I didn't expect to see you! What brings you here?"
"I've heard so much about you." (سمعت عنك الكثير)

Context: Use this when meeting a friend's friend or a colleague you've heard of.

  • "Finally! I've heard so much about you from Sarah."
  • "I've heard so much about you. It's nice to meet you."
  • "I've heard so much about your project. Good job!"
"What do you do for a living?" (ما هو عملك؟)

Context: The standard way to ask about someone's job.

  • "So, what do you do for a living?"
  • "It's a pleasure meeting you. What do you do for a living?"
  • "What do you do for a living? Are you in tech?"
"How about yourself?" (وأنت؟ / ماذا عنك؟)

Context: A more polite/formal version of "How about you?"

  • "I work in sales. How about yourself?"
  • "I'm really into jazz. How about yourself?"
  • "I'm based in Tangier. How about yourself?"

Natural Dialogues

1. At a Social Event
A: This place is beautiful! I'm Leo, by the way.
B: I'm Maya. Nice to meet you, Leo. What brings you here?
A: I'm a friend of the host. We went to school together.
B: Oh, cool! I'm originally from the same hometown as him.
A: Small world! And what do you do for a living?
B: I'm big on graphic design, so I work as a freelancer.
2. First Day at Work
A: Hello! You must be the new developer. I'm Sarah.
B: Hi Sarah! Yes, I'm Omar. I've heard so much about you.
A: All good things, I hope! Are you based in this office?
B: Actually, I'm working remotely most of the time.
A: Nice. Are you keen on coffee? We have a great machine.
B: Definitely. Lead the way!

Common Pitfalls

1. I have 30 years.

1. I am 30 years old / I'm 30.

Why? English uses "to be" for age.

2. I am work as teacher.

2. I work as a teacher / I am a teacher.

Why? Never say "I am work". Also, always use "a/an" for jobs.

3. Nice to meet you. (Response: I am too.)

3. Nice to meet you. (Response: You too! / Likewise.)

Why? "I am too" doesn't make sense here. "You too" is short for "Nice to meet you too."
